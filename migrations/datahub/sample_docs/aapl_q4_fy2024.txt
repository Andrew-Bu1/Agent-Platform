Apple Inc. Q4 FY2024 Earnings Report
Fiscal Quarter Ended September 28, 2024

FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS

Apple Inc. reported Q4 FY2024 revenue of $94.9 billion, representing a 6% year-over-year increase. This exceeded analyst expectations of $94.0 billion. Quarterly earnings per share (EPS) reached $1.64, up 12% compared to Q4 FY2023. The company's strong performance was driven by growth across all geographic segments, with Services revenue achieving an all-time high.

IPHONE

iPhone revenue for Q4 FY2024 was $46.2 billion, up 6% year-over-year, driven by strong demand for the iPhone 16 lineup. The iPhone 16 Pro and Pro Max models were particularly popular due to their Apple Intelligence features. iPhone unit sales saw improvement in emerging markets including India and Southeast Asia, where Apple continued to expand its retail presence.

SERVICES

Services revenue reached an all-time quarterly high of $25.0 billion in Q4 FY2024, growing 13% year-over-year. The segment includes the App Store, Apple Music, Apple TV+, iCloud, and Apple Pay. The installed base of active devices surpassed 2.2 billion units globally. Gross margin for Services was 74%, significantly higher than the product margin of 35.3%.

MAC AND IPAD

Mac revenue was $7.7 billion in Q4 FY2024, up 2% year-over-year, supported by M4 chip refresh cycles. iPad revenue grew 8% to $7.0 billion following the launch of the M4 iPad Pro and updated iPad Air. Wearables, Home, and Accessories revenue was $9.0 billion, relatively flat compared to the prior year period.

GUIDANCE AND CAPITAL RETURNS

Apple provided Q1 FY2025 guidance of $125-127 billion in revenue, implying approximately 5% growth. The company returned $29 billion to shareholders through dividends and buybacks during Q4. Total cash and marketable securities stood at $156 billion. The board authorized an additional $110 billion share repurchase program.

GEOGRAPHIC PERFORMANCE

Americas revenue grew 7% to $41.3 billion. Europe revenue grew 3% to $21.0 billion. Greater China revenue declined 1% to $15.0 billion due to competitive pressure in the premium smartphone market. Japan and Rest of Asia Pacific grew 2% and 9% respectively.

CEO COMMENTARY

"This quarter's results speak to Apple's enduring strength and the remarkable loyalty of our customers," said Tim Cook, Apple's CEO. "Apple Intelligence is the beginning of an exciting new chapter, and its features are driving strong interest in iPhone 16."
