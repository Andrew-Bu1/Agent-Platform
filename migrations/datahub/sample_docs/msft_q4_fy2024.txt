Microsoft Corporation Q4 FY2024 Earnings Report
Fiscal Quarter Ended June 30, 2024

FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS

Microsoft reported Q4 FY2024 revenue of $64.7 billion, up 15% year-over-year, exceeding consensus estimates of $64.4 billion. Net income grew 10% to $22.0 billion. EPS came in at $2.95, above the analyst estimate of $2.93. The company's AI-driven cloud growth continued to be the primary growth driver across all segments.

INTELLIGENT CLOUD

The Intelligent Cloud segment, which includes Azure, SQL Server, Windows Server, and GitHub, generated revenue of $28.5 billion, growing 19% year-over-year. Azure and other cloud services grew 29%, with approximately 8 percentage points of that growth attributable to AI services. The Azure AI Foundry platform saw rapid adoption from enterprise customers integrating OpenAI models into their workflows.

PRODUCTIVITY AND BUSINESS PROCESSES

Microsoft 365 Commercial revenue grew 13% to $20.3 billion in Q4 FY2024. Copilot M365 seats continued to expand with over 400,000 organizations using Copilot. LinkedIn revenue grew 10%. Dynamics products and cloud services grew 16%, driven by Dynamics 365 Copilot adoption. The Productivity and Business Processes segment totaled $26.5 billion.

GAMING AND MORE PERSONAL COMPUTING

Gaming revenue grew 61% to $5.5 billion, primarily driven by the Activision Blizzard acquisition contribution. Xbox content and services revenue grew 61%. Microsoft provided Q1 FY2025 revenue guidance of $63.8-64.8 billion. Capital expenditures were $19 billion in Q4, reflecting ongoing investment in AI infrastructure and data center capacity.

AI STRATEGY

Microsoft's Copilot ecosystem is expanding across all product lines. GitHub Copilot now has over 1.8 million paid subscribers. Azure OpenAI Service customers exceeded 60,000. The company committed to $55 billion in capital expenditure for FY2025, primarily to build AI infrastructure.

OPERATING METRICS

Operating income was $27.9 billion, representing a 43.1% operating margin. Free cash flow was $23.3 billion. The company returned $8.4 billion to shareholders through dividends and share buybacks. Commercial remaining performance obligations grew 19% to $269 billion.
